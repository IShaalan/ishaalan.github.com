WinjiGo - Social Learning Platform

A social learning platform designed for next-generation teaching and learning. It enables different teaching strategies such as flipped, blended, and inquiry based learning. WinjiGo provides teachers with gamification tools to foster learners' skills and address their specific needs within an engaging education environment.

Context

Traditionally, Edtech solutions were designed as non-intrusive, supportive, and enriching solutions that wouldn't disrupt the traditional classroom experience. However, this notion changed in February 2020 when everyone started to realize the effects that CoVid is going to have. Students were forced to stay at home and educators were not ready to pivot to a remote-first learning environment. In short, as many industries, the education sector was suddenly disrupted.

Project Goal

Redesign WinjiGo's experience to enable teachers and students to effectively use the platform as their main tool and hub for teaching and learning.

My Role

As the product manager for the app, I worked on:
  • Led the UX revamp of the platform to adopt the users new reality
  • Grew the value of WinjiGo with our customers exponentially by building partnerships with 12 content providers and integrating seamlessly with their content/platforms

Results

  • Launched the revamped platform in 5 weeks
  • Our customers were able to continue education uninterruptedly
  • In UAE, 250,000 students completed more than 500,000 hours of learning on their first day of distant learning during CoVid Pandemic. Read more
  • Grew WinjiGo's user base 40% in 2 months

Case Studies

Read more about the successfull implementations:
  • Ministy of Education - United Arab Emirates
  • Alekhaa Private School - Kingdom of Saudi Arabia
  • Egyptian Japanese Schools - Egypt

TeacherKit

A swiss army knife for teachers to simplify administrative tasks and facilitate in-classroom experience.

My Role

As the product manager for the app, I worked on:
  • Envisioning the product strategy, roadmap, and go-to-market plan
  • Designing the monetization strategy
  • Leading the development and quality teams
  • Designed the technology strategy for the rollout, implementation, and governance of the technology backbone

Origin Story

TeacherKit was published as a free app on the App Store to as an R&D initiative and to demonstrate the team's capabilities. The app was the first B2C product in ITWORX's history. After getting some traction on the store, the company decided to capitalize on this momentum by scaling and monetize the app.

Results

  • 80% increase in average daily downloads in the teacher app
  • Grew the user base by more than 8 times in 1 year
  • Converted 12.5% of the user base upon releasing the premium features

Notable Reviews

  • "Does it deliver? Yes. TeacherKit delivers with flying colors and we mean that literally. The app is one of the crispest looking apps that are available for teachers and its visual appeal is only one of its many pluses. Everything the app promises is available for teachers."
  • "I was initially a little skeptical about TeacherKit because it sounded too good to be true. However, it really is that great! I could think of only a few cons, and many pros. The developers for TeacherKit clearly have a good sense of what teachers need in order to help classroom management and organization."

GEMS Education

Context

GEMS Education is the largest private school operator in the world. They grew and acquired different private schools around the world serving different customer segments with tuition ranging from $200 per year to $50,000. Each school had its own IT budget and software solutions – most of them are not being used. As the number of schools grew the budget grew exponentially and managing the sheer number of solutions became impossible resulting in a degradation of service for all stakeholders.

Project Goal

GEMS Education needed to achieve the following objectives before their anticipated IPO offering:
  • Decrease the annual IT spending
  • Have a single source of truth for the whole organization
  • Improve the users' satisfaction
  • Create differentiated offerings for the different customer segments
  • Increase the adoption of products

My Role

  • Interviewed GEMS leadership team and stakeholders from all departments of the schools across 6 countries
  • Developed a toolkit to evaluate the maturity of education technology of the schools
  • Shaped the unified technology backbone that allows for adding customized blocks (i.e. products) that meets the localized needs of the different segments
  • Designed the technology strategy for the rollout, implementation, and governance of the technology backbone

Results

  • Drastic reduction in the number of applications from over 1000 distinct application to less than 200 (including content apps)
  • 40% Reduction in the IT spending per year starting from the first year of the implementation
